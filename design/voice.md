# Voice

Source: Brand Book §08 + Design System §08.

Two voices, one room. UI copy is shorter than it wants to be,
lower-case more often than not, and assumes the reader is an adult
in a relationship — not a customer.

## In-app voice

Quiet. Warm. Certain. Lowercase. Modern.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| `tap for next` | `→ Continue, dearest reader` |
| `tap for next` | `→ Continue to the next question` |
| `that's all of them.` | `🎉 You completed the deck` |
| `choose a register.` | `Pick a category to get started` |
| `not this one. try again.` | `⚠ Invalid invitation key` |

## Marketing voice

A single regency salutation per page, never more. The Whistledown
letterhead is reserved for releases and changelogs.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| `Dearest reader, ask each other this.` | `Spice up date night with prompts!` |
| `Conversation, between us.` | `The #1 couples' question app` |

## Rules

1. **Salutation.** `Dearest reader,` appears at most once per page,
   only in marketing surfaces, never in-app.
2. **No emoji.** Anywhere. Including marketing. The italic does the
   smiling.
3. **Lowercase.** UI strings. Deck names. Headlines on the holding
   page. (Italics for warmth, not capitals.)
4. **Periods earn it.** Use full stops on UI lines that *are*
   sentences. They land.
5. **Italic carries the human.** Use italics for the warm word in a
   sentence (*truly*, *us*, *together*) — never for emphasis on a
   verb of navigation.
6. **Sentence length.** UI copy is shorter than it wants to be. If a
   line restates the obvious, cut it.
7. **No customer-speak.** Never address the reader as a user, a
   customer, or a fan. Address them as one of two adults in a
   relationship.

## Posture choice (where each voice lives)

- **A · Read straight** — in-app, App Store screenshots, newsletter
  body. Wordmark + small italic lede (`Conversation, between us.`).
- **B · Lean in lightly** — holding-page hero, launch announcement,
  press kit, the seal favicon. Adds the seal monogram and a regency
  line. Once or twice — never every surface.

The wink earns its keep when it's rare.
