This is the working brand book for Genuine Gossip — a conversation-starter app for couples. The name is a wink to the regency-letter voice of Bridgerton; the product is a quiet card you pass back and forth. This document defines the wordmark, monogram, brand color, app icon, voice, and a holding page for genuinegossip.app.

01 / The name

Why Genuine Gossip.

It does too much work to be coincidence — a wink for fans, a promise for everyone else.

The wink

A regency catchphrase from Bridgerton's fourth season — the kind of phrase Lady Whistledown would print and the ton would whisper. It belongs to fans without belonging to the show.

The promise

Gossip implies trust, intimacy, and the small thrill of telling someone the truth. Genuine rules out the cheap kind. The product writes to that bar.

The clearance

No App Store / Play / USPTO collisions. Domain genuinegossip.app available. The Bridgerton phrase is descriptive, not protected — no Netflix / Shondaland exposure.

02 / Monogram

Five forms of gg.

The app icon needs a mark, not a wordmark. Five takes — pick one, mix two.

A · italic gg
B · single g.
C · stacked Gg
D · seal
E · ligature
Recommendation

Form A — the italic gg ligature — is the everyday mark. It survives at favicon size, sits nicely on a tinted square, and doesn't tip into pastiche. Form D (the wax seal) is held in reserve for letterhead, the changelog, and the holding page hero — a gentler regency wink.

04 / Tone

Read straight, or lean in.

Same wordmark, two postures. The product reads straight; the brand surfaces (site, store, social) can lean a little.

Construction

The italic gg sits on the optical center, not the geometric center — nudge up 4% of the canvas. Cap-height is 60% of the canvas. The descender of the second g ends at the lower clear-space line.
