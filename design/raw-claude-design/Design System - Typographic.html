The product is a single card held between two people. Type does the work. The interface stays out of the way.

This document is the working spec for Two of Us — a conversation-starter app for couples in private early access. It defines the type scale, color, space, motion, and components needed to ship a product that feels less like an app and more like an object you'd keep on a nightstand. Everything here is opinionated on purpose.

01 / Principles

Five rules before
any pixel.

Decisions worth defending. If a design move violates one of these, the move loses — not the rule.

i.

Text is the product.

Every prompt has been written, edited, and re-edited. Treat that work like a poem on a wall — frame it, don't decorate it. The card screen is mostly empty on purpose.

ii.

Quiet beats clever.

No micro-interactions for their own sake. No gradient flourishes. No mascot, no emoji, no ornament. Calm is the brand.

iii.

One typeface, two voices.

Cormorant Garamond carries every reading moment. Inter Tight handles labels, status, navigation. Italics carry warmth — use them.

iv.

Hide the chrome.

The card screen has one labelled control — tap for next. Everything else (change deck, restart) hides at the edges and reveals on dwell. Reading first.

v.

Light and dark are equal.

This is not a dark-mode-as-skin app. Both modes are designed; both are tested with real card text. The dark mode is for couches and beds. Honor it.

vi.

If in doubt, take it out.

The pull is always toward more — more content, more reassurance, more affordance. Resist. The product is a single sentence and the room around it.

04 / Space

Roomy on purpose.

A 4-pixel base, eased into a near-geometric scale. Use the large end of the scale liberally — the silence around a card is part of what the card means.

Mobile margins

Phone screens use 22px horizontal gutters and 46px top safe-area inset. Card text sits in a 22 / 84 / 22 / 110 inset (T R B L → top 84, bottom 110) so the prompt is always vertically centered with breathing room above and below the controls.

Tap targets

Minimum 44 × 44 px. The card screen is the exception: the entire screen is the tap target for "next." Edge handles for change/restart are 56 px wide and reveal on dwell.

06 / Motion

Slow.
Honest.

Motion is the difference between feeling like an object and feeling like an interface. Default to under-doing it — a card change is a fade and a half-second, not a flick.

durations

240 · 380 · 600 ms

240 for state changes (button hover, ruler tick). 380 for card advance. 600 for screen-to-screen. Nothing snaps.

easing

cubic-bezier (.2, .7, .2, 1)

Decelerating ease for everything. No bounce, no overshoot, no spring. The product never arrives — it settles.

card transition

crossfade · no slide

New card cross-fades over the old at 380ms. No horizontal motion — the card is a held object, not a swiped page.

Reduce motion

When the OS reports prefers-reduced-motion, drop all transitions to opacity-only fades at 120ms. The product still changes states, just instantly.

Haptics

A single soft haptic on card advance. Nothing on deck pick, nothing on gate enter. Two of Us never buzzes for confirmation.
